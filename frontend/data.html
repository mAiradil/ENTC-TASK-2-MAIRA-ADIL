Total Orders Comparison: Manual vs. Online System


Detailed Analysis


This chart visually compares the total number of food orders placed using the manual ordering system versus the online ordering system over a four-week period. As seen in the chart, the number of orders during the manual ordering phase remains relatively low and stable each week, with only minor fluctuations. This trend reflects the inefficiency of the manual system, where students may have avoided ordering due to long queues, time limitations, or lack of awareness.


The visualisation makes it easy to see that the transition to an online sys tem not only boosted total sales but also improved engagement with the canteen's services. By streamlining the ordering process, the canteen could handle more orders efficiently, reduce congestion, and increase student satisfaction. This insight supports the strategic decision to continue investing in digital solutions for school services.



Average Number of Orders: Manual vs. Online



Average Orders Breakdown


This bar graph shows a simple and clear comparison of the average number of orders placed using both the manual system and the online ordering system. The height of each bar visually highlights the huge improvement that occurred when the online system was introduced.


On the left, the manual system's bar is much shorter, representing fewer average orders per week. This shows that not many students consistently ordered food when it was done manually. On the right, the bar for the online system is much taller, which shows how online ordering made it easier and more appealing for students to order regularly.


Because the graph is so simple and easy to read, it makes the point very clearly: switching to online ordering boosted the average weekly order volume by a large margin. This proves how effective and useful the new system is for both students and the canteen.



Dashboard Overview: Orders, Trends & Averages


What This Dashboard Shows


This interactive dashboard brings everything together, it combines data from total sales, weekly trends, and average order volumes to give a complete picture of how the canteen's performance changed after moving to an online system.


On one screen, you can quickly compare how different food categories performed, track changes over time, and see just how much more efficient the online system is. This makes it super easy to identify patterns, highlight improvements, and support future decision-making with real data.


The dashboard layout is clear and user-friendly. Each section gives a different type of insight, from how much students order each day, to which system gets more orders on average. It's a great way to understand what's working and where the canteen can grow next!
