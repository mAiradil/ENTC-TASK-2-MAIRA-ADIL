Data Insights: Understanding Canteen Orders

Total Orders Comparison: Manual vs. Online System

Detailed Analysis

This chart visually compares the total number of food orders placed using the manual ordering system versus the online ordering system over a four-week period. As seen in the chart, the number of orders during the manual ordering phase remains relatively low and stable each week, with only minor fluctuations. This trend reflects the inefficiency of the manual system, where students may have avoided ordering due to long queues, time limitations, or lack of awareness.

The visualisation makes it easy to see that the transition to an online system not only boosted total sales but also improved engagement with the canteen's services. By streamlining the ordering process, the canteen could handle more orders efficiently, reduce congestion, and increase student satisfaction. This insight supports the strategic decision to continue investing in digital solutions for school services.


Average Number of Orders: Manual vs. Online

Average Orders Breakdown

This bar graph shows a simple and clear comparison of the average number of orders placed using both the manual system and the online ordering system. The height of each bar visually highlights the huge improvement that occurred when the online system was introduced.

On the left, the manual system's bar is much shorter, representing fewer average orders per week. This shows that not many students consistently ordered food when it was done manually. On the right, the bar for the online system is much taller, which shows how online ordering made it easier and more appealing for students to order regularly.

Because the graph is so simple and easy to read, it makes the point very clearly: switching to online ordering boosted the average weekly order volume by a large margin. This proves how effective and useful the new system is for both students and the canteen.


Most Popular Canteen Items

Student Food Preferences in Detail

This visualisation showcases the most popular items ordered from the school canteen, broken down by ordering method, manual versus online. The stacked bars allow us to clearly see not only which items are favourites, but also how ordering behaviour has shifted between systems.

Items like Meat Pies and Mince Nachos top the chart, demonstrating strong overall popularity. However, the visual difference between the blue (manual) and orange (online) segments highlights a key trend: more students are choosing to order these items online. This shift suggests that popular products benefit the most from being available in a faster, digital format.

This graph tells an important story about what students actually want. The data is meaningful because it proves demand patterns, supports menu planning, and shows how digital ordering increases accessibility to in-demand food. It also helps reduce waste by helping the canteen predict what items to stock more of based on ordering method.

Overall, this data backs up the success of the online system and provides real insights that can help the school improve both the student experience and the efficiency of the canteen.


Dashboard Overview: Orders, Trends & Averages

What This Dashboard Shows

This interactive dashboard brings everything together. It combines data from total sales, weekly trends, and average order volumes to give a complete picture of how the canteen's performance changed after moving to an online system.

On one screen, you can quickly compare how different food categories performed, track changes over time, and see just how much more efficient the online system is. This makes it super easy to identify patterns, highlight improvements, and support future decision-making with real data.

The dashboard layout is clear and user-friendly. Each section gives a different type of insight, from how much students order each day, to which system gets more orders on average. It's a great way to understand what's working and where the canteen can grow next!



Survey Insights: Understanding Our Users

Who Are Our Users?

Who are our users?

Over 84% of respondents are students, this is a huge insight that anchors everything. It tells us that our solution must be student first, fast, accessible, and adapted to student routines. Understanding our audience means designing a system that meets their real-life pressures like time limits, busy schedules, and convenience.

Canteen Usage Frequency

Canteen Usage Frequency

This chart shows that only 5% of students use the canteen daily, while a surprising 42% rarely use it. That's a red flag. It suggests that the current system isn't engaging or efficient enough. This data tells the story of a broken experience, one where students might be avoiding the canteen due to long waits, lack of flexibility, or poor menu visibility. It highlights a clear need for solutions like pre-ordering systems, live menus, or digital tokens, that make the canteen more inviting and useful.

Rating the Manual Ordering System


Rating the Manual Ordering System

With most users giving low ratings (1-3), it's clear that the current manual system is not meeting expectations. This question directly supports the need for an upgrade, backing up our hypothesis that students want a faster, more convenient way to order food, like an online system.

What's Wrong With the Current System?


What's Wrong With the Current System?

This open-ended question is the heart of our data. It captures voices of frustration which is the most common themes with having long lines, lack of pre-ordering, card machine failures, and unclear menus or halal options. These aren't just numbers.

The system is inefficient, outdated, and it's genuinely affecting students' ability to get food during short break times. This data supports the need for a digital solution, such as an online pre-order system with menu visibility and payment integration. It gives weight to the data shown in our charts and proves that change is not just helpful, it's necessary.

Would You Order Online?


Would You Order Online?

A massive 79% of users gave a 4 or 5 when asked if they'd use an online system. This isn't just a stat, it's a clear signal of demand. It tells a story of students who are ready for a digital upgrade. Whether it's saving time or avoiding queues, the appetite for change is strong. This data gives confidence that building an online canteen platform is needed.

Smart Recommendations Matter


Smart Recommendations Matter

68.4% rated a full 5 when asked about wanting a personalised food ordering experience. This insight speaks volumes — users want more than just functionality. They want systems that remember their preferences, offer dietary filters, and make ordering feel tailored and thoughtful. It shows that people are more likely to use tech when it feels like it "knows" them.

Involving Parents in Food Choices

Involving Parents in Food Choices

Almost 90% agree that online ordering helps parents guide their children's choices. That's powerful. It shifts the conversation from just student convenience to family-level empowerment. It adds emotional weight to the system — it's not just smart, it's inclusive. This data proves that transparency and shared decision-making could make the platform more trusted, respected, and widely adopted at home.

Is Food Waste a Significant Issue?

Is Food Waste a Significant Issue?

This data is deeply insightful in highlighting uncertainty and concern about food waste. With 42.1% of respondents saying "Yes" and another 42.1% saying "Not Sure," it paints a picture of a community that's either witnessing waste or lacks transparency about the issue. Only 15.8% believe it's not a problem. It also strengthens the case for digital innovation: a smart ordering system could track quantities, improve forecasting, and reduce waste. This chart reveals a hidden opportunity to build sustainability into the system.

Improving Budget Efficiency

Improving Budget Efficiency

A significant 84.2% of respondents believe that a food waste tracking system could help the canteen manage its budget more effectively. This strong consensus highlights a shared awareness that reducing over-ordering and minimising waste is not only sustainable but financially smart. The data suggests there is clear support for intelligent systems that enhance cost-efficiency without compromising quality.

Support for Smarter Meal Planning

Support for Smarter Meal Planning

With 84.2% of respondents in favour, there is strong support for using food waste tracking to improve how the canteen plans its meals. This indicates that users value data-driven planning and trust that such systems can lead to better decisions, reduce leftovers, and meet demand more accurately. It reflects a preference for thoughtful, sustainable meal management.

Student Support for Digital Solutions


Student Support for Digital Solutions

This chart shows that the majority of users believe introducing an online ordering system and a food waste tracker would be extremely useful. With nearly 85% rating it a 4 or 5 out of 5, the support is clear. This data confirms that students are ready for and want smarter systems that improve speed, convenience, and sustainability. It strengthens the case for a digital upgrade and aligns perfectly with the issues highlighted in the previous feedback.
